How does this work?

The Advanced Encryption Standard , also known as Rijndael, is a block cipher system adopted as an encryption standard by the United States government, although it was created in Belgium. It became an effective standard on May 26, 2002. Since 2006, AES has been one of the most popular algorithms used in symmetric cryptography. AES is faster than DES in both software and hardware and is simple to implement. As a new encryption standard, it is currently being used on a large scale.
