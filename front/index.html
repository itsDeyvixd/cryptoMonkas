A brief history ...

In modern days, cryptography heavily relies on mathematical theories and computer implementations; but it wasn't always this way.

The origins of cryptographic methods go all the way back to the ancient times. The spartans used the scytale consisting on a cilinder and a strip where the text was written on. The strip was wounded around the cilinder and the plain text was written from left to right (not in the direction of the winding). Unfolding the strip would give the cypher text.

Julius Caesar

invented another method of classic cypher known as the substitution cypher.

It consists on shifting every letter from the alphabet a fixed amount. For example, the emperor himself used a shifting of 3. in that case the A converts into D

Modern cryptography

Despite it’s etimological origins, the modern latin cryptographia wasn’t introduced until around the 1500. Johannes Trithemius is considered one of the fathers of modern cryptography due to his most famous work Steganographia (written around 1499), which looks like a text about using spirits to communicate over long distances; but is actually a cypher text (whose decryption key got published on 1606) concerned about cryptography and steganography
